# Project METIS — Executive Proposal

**An AI-powered delivery platform for professional services firms**

---

## The Problem

Professional services firms run on knowledge. Every engagement generates insights — what worked, what didn't, which configurations solve which problems, how to navigate specific client environments. But that knowledge lives in people's heads, scattered documents, and disconnected tools.

When a senior consultant leaves, their knowledge walks out the door. When a new engagement starts, the team rebuilds understanding from scratch — even if someone else solved the same problem six months ago. The firm's most valuable asset — its accumulated expertise — is invisible, fragile, and underused.

Meanwhile, the industry is shifting. Clients expect faster delivery, higher quality, and lower costs. AI is transforming how knowledge work gets done. The largest consulting firms have responded by investing billions in internal AI platforms. But mid-market firms — the ones that rely most on their people's expertise — have been left behind.

---

## What METIS Does

METIS is an AI platform that learns what your organisation knows — your products, your processes, your delivery history — and uses that knowledge to help your team deliver faster and better.

It is not a chatbot. It is not a search tool. It is a system that:

- **Learns your domain** — ingests product documentation, process guides, past engagement records, and external system data to build a living knowledge base
- **Works alongside your team** — AI agents assist consultants through structured delivery workflows, producing real artifacts (configurations, test plans, documentation) not just suggestions
- **Enforces quality** — every step follows governed processes with human checkpoints. The AI cannot skip steps or bypass quality gates
- **Gets smarter over time** — each engagement adds to the knowledge base. Patterns that work for one client can be promoted to benefit all future engagements. The system compounds in value

---

## Why Now

Three forces have converged to make this possible and urgent:

**1. AI capabilities have matured.** Large language models can now understand domain-specific context, follow multi-step processes, and produce production-quality work — not just answer questions. The technology is ready.

**2. The Big 4 have validated the model.** Every major consulting firm has built internal AI platforms (McKinsey's Lilli, Deloitte's PairD, PwC's tools, EY.ai). They've proven the concept works — AI that knows the domain can transform delivery speed and quality. But these platforms are locked inside walled gardens. Mid-market firms can't access them.

**3. The window is closing.** Platform vendors (Microsoft, Salesforce, ServiceNow) are rapidly expanding their AI capabilities. Within 18-24 months, "good enough" alternatives will emerge. The opportunity to establish a purpose-built platform for professional services — before the generalists arrive — is now.

---

## What Makes METIS Different

Most AI tools solve the "find information" problem. METIS solves the "use information to do governed work" problem.

### Knowledge That Compounds

Every engagement teaches METIS something. A configuration pattern that works for Client A can be generalised and made available for future engagements — without exposing client-specific data. Over time, the platform accumulates institutional knowledge that no individual consultant carries. This is the core value proposition: **the more you use it, the more valuable it becomes.**

### Governed Execution

AI agents in METIS follow the same quality processes as your people. Every delivery workflow has defined stages, human checkpoints, and quality gates. The AI cannot skip a step, bypass a review, or escalate without human approval. This is not a limitation — it is a feature. Governance is what makes AI trustworthy enough for client-facing work.

### Your Knowledge, Your Control

Each customer's data is fully isolated. Knowledge separates cleanly into product-level (shared across engagements) and client-level (private to the engagement). Nothing leaks sideways. Customers own their institutional knowledge — METIS is the platform, not the owner.

## Use Cases

### Assisted Delivery

A consultant starts a new engagement. Instead of spending weeks learning the product and client context, METIS already knows — it has the product documentation, the delivery methodology, and the lessons from previous engagements. The AI assists through each stage: requirements gathering, configuration, testing, documentation, and handoff.

### Intelligent Defect Management

A defect is reported. METIS captures it in natural language, structures it automatically, checks for duplicates across the engagement and across the organisation, links it to the relevant systems (e.g., Jira), and tracks it through resolution. Patterns emerge across engagements — the same issue appearing for multiple clients triggers a product-level investigation.

### Knowledge-Powered Onboarding

A new client is onboarded. METIS ingests their specific context (tools, processes, terminology), creates a tailored AI assistant constrained to their domain, and validates it before deployment. The onboarding process itself follows a governed workflow — nothing goes live without verification.

### Continuous Improvement

As engagements complete, METIS identifies which knowledge items were most useful, which processes could be improved, and which patterns should be promoted from client-specific to product-wide. The system doesn't just deliver — it learns from delivery.

## The Opportunity for Nimbus

METIS is designed to be used by professional services firms — and nimbus is a professional services firm. The first and most immediate application is nimbus itself:

- **Faster engagement delivery** — consultants spend less time on context-gathering and more time on value-adding work
- **Preserved institutional knowledge** — expertise is captured in the platform, not lost when people move on
- **Consistent quality** — governed processes ensure every engagement follows the same standard, regardless of who delivers it
- **Scalable capacity** — AI-assisted delivery means the same team can handle more work without proportional headcount growth
- **Competitive differentiation** — offering AI-augmented delivery is a genuine market differentiator for clients evaluating PS partners

Beyond internal use, there is a commercial opportunity to offer the platform to other mid-market PS firms — but that is a later conversation. The first step is proving value internally.
